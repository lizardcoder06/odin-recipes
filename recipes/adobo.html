Adobong Manok (Chicken Adobo)

Description

Adobong Manok is a classic Filipino chicken dish simmered in a savory blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper. This easy-to-make recipe delivers tender, flavorful chicken with a rich, tangy sauce perfect for pairing with steamed rice. Simple, hearty, and deeply satisfying!

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 onion (optional, sliced)
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional, for a hint of sweetness)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the garlic (and onions if using) until fragrant.
  3. Add the marinated chicken (reserve the marinade) and sear until lightly browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the reserved marinade, add bay leaves, and bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for about 30–40 minutes or until the chicken is tender. Add water if the sauce reduces too much.
  6. Optional: Add brown sugar for a touch of sweetness and stir well.
  7. Uncover and continue to simmer until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
