Tinola na Manok (Chicken Tinola)

Description

Tinola na Manok is a comforting Filipino chicken soup traditionally made with chicken, ginger, green papaya, and chili leaves. The broth is light yet flavorful, with a delicate combination of ginger, fish sauce, and pepper. Perfect for rainy days or whenever you're in need of a warm, nourishing meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2–3 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 2-inch piece of ginger (sliced thinly)
  • 1 medium green papaya (peeled, seeded, and cut into wedges)
  • 1 bunch of chili leaves (or malunggay/moringa leaves, optional)
  • 1–2 long green chilies (optional, for heat)
  • 6 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant and the onions become translucent.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in the water or chicken broth, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, lower the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the chicken is tender.
  4. Add the fish sauce and pepper, and adjust seasoning with salt if needed.
  5. Add the green papaya and continue simmering for another 10-15 minutes or until the papaya is tender.
  6. Lastly, add the chili leaves (or malunggay leaves) and green chilies. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  7. Taste the broth and adjust seasoning as necessary, adding more fish sauce or salt if desired.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice, and enjoy the warming and flavorful Tinola!
